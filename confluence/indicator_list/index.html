4. List of Indicators

The Indicator List is used to define the category/indicator/sub-indicator triple along with the severity (1 = minimum and 5 = maximum) for each risk category. These category/indicator/sub-indicator triples provide a mechanism to combine together reports for various risks.

Consider the example listed in the table below:

Country Sector Category Indicator Sub-indicator Severity (5=extreme, 1= low)
All All Labor Child labor Children within facility premises but not working 1
All Cotton Labor Abusive working and living conditions Accommodation does not comply with health, safety, and occupation requirements 3
All Cotton Occupational Safety & Health Worker protection Workers are punished if they remove themselves from work situations that they believe present an imminent and serious danger to health 5

When reports are received from ESG Assessors, the risk associated with the reported case (low=1; medium=2; high=3) is combined with the severity from this indicator list, enabling an overall risk to be calculated from different reports and different sources.

The indicator list is uploaded by the super administrator during system configuration using an excel template. Once successfully uploaded, the super administrator can also uploaded a translated version of the list, downloading the translation template, completing it, and then uploading.
