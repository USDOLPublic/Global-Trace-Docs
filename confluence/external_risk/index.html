Global Trace

Appendix A. External risk

Global Trace supports external risk indices to be defined for countries. This risk data must be input into the system using an excel template, available in the following directory.

src/risk-assessments/constants/goods-risk.json

Country risk

The first sheet enables the super administrator to input initial values for country risk. In our sample workbook, the risk is defined as a combination of Global Slavery Index ratings and US State Department’s Trafficking in Persons Report Tier.

This document lists each country and provides a risk rating on a scale of 0 to 100 (0 is low risk, 100 is extreme risk).

Country Risk (out of 100)
Afghanistan 95
Albania 36
Algeria 72

The second sheet provides a country / commodity risk rating. Each category / Indicator / Sub-indicator must also appear in the Indicator list, enabling the rating data to be combined in the overall supplier risk. In our example, we use the sub-indicator field to show the source of the rating. As with ESG assessment reports, the severity is marked on a 3 point scale (1: low, 2: high, 3: extreme)

Country/ Area Good Category Indicator Sub-Indicator Severity
Afghanistan Bricks Labor Forced Labor Goods produced with forced labor (ILAB List of Goods) 3
Afghanistan Bricks Labor Child Labor Goods produced with child labor (ILAB List of Goods) 3
