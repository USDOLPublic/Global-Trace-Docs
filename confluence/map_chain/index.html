3. Mapping the supply chain

The supply chain map helps to define the sale and purchase relationships between producer roles in the application.

Each node in the map contains a role, a from-role, and an output product. This describes that the role can buy from the from-role and can sell the output product to the role of its next node. The output product that a role can buy is determined by its previous node.

In addition, it can also show that the two roles of two linked nodes can trade with each other through intermediaries (brokers). A double vertical bar will be displayed to represent that trading via intermediaries is possible if the role of node has permission ALLOW_PURCHASE_INTERMEDIARIES/ ALLOW_SALE_INTERMEDIARIES.

Info

Example: This map illustrates a chain of four roles: farm, ginner, spinner, and fabric mill. Ginners can sell their lots to spinners, spinners can buy lots from ginners. The double vertical bar in the middle of their linked line also indicates that they can not only buy and sell directly with each other but can also buy and sell through intermediaries. The ginner sells the lot to an intermediary then the spinner can buy this lot from the intermediary. But the farm can only directly trade with the ginner.

The rules for creating a node are as follows:

  • role and from-role must be product roles (role type is PRODUCT).
  • from-role of the first node or the initial node can be null. This role is usually a non-participating, role that doesn’t have any users and doesn’t login to the system.
  • role of a node must have permission LOG_PURCHASE.
  • from-role of a node except to first node must have permission LOG_SALE.
